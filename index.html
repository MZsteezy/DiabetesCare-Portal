Continuous Glucose Monitoring (CGM)

A sensor tracks blood sugar levels 24/7, sending data to an app for both patients and healthcare providers.

Real-Time Alerts

Receive notifications for high or low blood sugar levels to help prevent emergencies.

Benefits

For Patients

  • Fewer manual glucose checks
  • Early warnings to prevent complications

For Healthcare Providers

  • Instant access to patient data
  • Ability to intervene early, reducing hospital visits

Diabetes Management Tips

Healthy Eating Tips

  • Balanced Diet: Focus on eating a variety of nutrient-dense foods, including whole grains, lean proteins, healthy fats, fruits, and vegetables.
  • Portion Control: Use smaller plates and be mindful of serving sizes to manage blood sugar levels effectively.
  • Low Glycemic Index Foods: Opt for foods like legumes, oats, and non-starchy vegetables that release energy slowly.
  • Limit Sugary and Processed Foods: Reduce intake of sugary drinks, desserts, and highly processed snacks.
  • Meal Timing: Eat meals at consistent times each day to regulate blood sugar.

Understanding Insulin Management

  • Know Your Insulin Types: Understand the difference between rapid-acting, intermediate, and long-acting insulin.
  • Monitor Blood Sugar Regularly: Track levels before meals and bedtime as recommended.
  • Injection Sites: Rotate injection sites to prevent skin irritation or thickening.
  • Carbohydrate Counting: Learn how to match insulin doses to carbohydrate intake for better control.
  • Consult a Specialist: Work closely with your healthcare provider to adjust insulin dosage as needed.

Exercise for Diabetes

  • Aerobic Exercise: Activities like walking, swimming, or cycling for at least 30 minutes a day, five days a week.
  • Strength Training: Incorporate resistance exercises twice a week to improve muscle strength and glucose uptake.
  • Consistency is Key: Exercise regularly to maintain stable blood sugar levels.
  • Monitor Blood Sugar: Check glucose levels before and after exercise, especially if taking insulin.
  • Stay Hydrated: Drink water to prevent dehydration during physical activity.

Stress and Diabetes Education

  • Understand the Connection: Stress can elevate blood sugar levels due to the release of stress hormones like cortisol.
  • Stress Management Techniques: Practice mindfulness, meditation, or yoga to reduce stress levels.
  • Support Groups: Engage with diabetes support groups to share experiences and learn coping strategies.
  • Time Management: Organize daily routines to reduce overwhelming feelings and avoid stress-induced spikes in blood sugar.
  • Education Workshops: Attend diabetes management classes to build confidence in handling stress and managing the condition effectively.
